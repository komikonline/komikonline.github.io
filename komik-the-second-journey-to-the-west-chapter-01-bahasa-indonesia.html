4. A student runs 60 meter to the east in 60 second then 20 meter to the west in 40 second determine : a). average speed ( in m/s ) b). average velocity ( in m/s )​


Jawaban:

Penjelasan:

To calculate the average speed and average velocity, we need to consider the distance traveled and the time taken in each case.

Given:

Distance to the east = 60 meters

Time to the east = 60 seconds

Distance to the west = 20 meters

Time to the west = 40 seconds

Let's calculate:

a) Average Speed:

Average speed is the total distance traveled divided by the total time taken.

Total distance = Distance to the east + Distance to the west

Total distance = 60 meters + 20 meters = 80 meters

Total time = Time to the east + Time to the west

Total time = 60 seconds + 40 seconds = 100 seconds

Average speed = Total distance / Total time

Average speed = 80 meters / 100 seconds

Average speed = 0.8 m/s

b) Average Velocity:

Average velocity is the total displacement divided by the total time taken.

Displacement is the difference between the initial position and final position. Since the student starts and finishes at the same point, the displacement is zero (no change in position).

Total time (same as before) = 100 seconds

Average velocity = Displacement / Total time

Average velocity = 0 meters (displacement) / 100 seconds

Average velocity = 0 m/s

Therefore:

a) The average speed is 0.8 m/s.

b) The average velocity is 0 m/s.

15. West Irian Joined Republic of Indonesia The liberation of West Irian was one of the biggest issues of sovereignty at the beginning of the independence period of the Republic of Indonesia. This conflict arose when the Dutch colonialist was unwilling to let West Irian go into the NKRI section. The Indonesian government struggled through various methods ranging from diplomacy to the military. On December 19, 1961, the government issued “Tri Komando Rakyat (Trikora)” statements which determined to prevent the formation of a Papuan puppet state mad by the Dutch, to hoist the Indonesia flag in the entire West Irian area, and to prepare for general mobilization to maintain the independence and unity of the homeland and the nation. Ever since then, the confrontation with the Dutch began. On January 2, 1962, President Soekarno issued Decree No. 1 of 1962 to form the Mandala Command for Liberation of West Irian. In 1969, a referendum was held for the West Irian people. The result stated that the people in West Irian wanted to join the Republic of Indonesia. The success of “Trikora” was the result of military and diplomacy cooperation. On May 4, 1963, three days after the return of West Irian to Indonesia, President Soekarno addressed a crowd of thousands of West Irian people in Kota Baru, Jayapura stating that West Irian joined the Republic of Indonesia.
